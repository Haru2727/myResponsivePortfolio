Origin Story
Name:
Arash Saeedi


Name Origin:

Named after Arash e Kamangir, translated into Arash the Archer, my name comes from an old folk tale told for many generations in Iran. Thousands of years ago, a legendary bowman named Arash was sent by the shah to fire an arrow from the top of a mountain to redfine the Persian Empires border. The mighty archer waited til dawn to fire, giving it everything he had. The archer sacrficed himself, he put so much into the shot that he died instantly. The arrow traveled a thousand leagues before landing. This story helped fuel the passion for the Persian empire and had such an impact that until today many children are still named after the legendary hero.

Location:
Atlanta, GA


How Atlanta became home:

My father immigrated here from Iran in the 1970's. During those times most immigrants from Iran either went to Los Angeles, or to New York City. He chose NYC which is how I grew up in New Jersey for the first part of my life. From 1984 til 1999 I lived in New Jersey. Some of my fondest memories of those times are when i used to go to work with my dad and we got to be in the city all day, enjoying the diverse choices of food and the freedom that only a child of the 90's can understand. I got to explore and venture off here and there at time before cell phones and having to be on constant contact. In the late 90's he came into some hard times up there and with the advice and job offer from a close friend he decided it was time to move the family to Alpharetta, Ga. My first few months in the south had a shell shock effect on me. In the north everything is so compact I could walk or ride my bicycle anywhere a child could need. In Alpharetta that was not the case. I slowly adapted to my new surroundings especially once I got my drivers lisence. After college and a few years of saving I bought myself a condo in Atlanta. I bought into a neighborhood that allows me to walk or bike to anything I need. Atlanta feels like home more than ever now.
